# Canonical Curriculum

Source scope: Stanford University, *Introduction to Statistics* on Coursera, public course outline accessed 2026-07-12. Treat lesson titles as scope labels, not content to reproduce.

## Modules

1. **Introduction and Descriptive Statistics for Exploring Data**
   - Pie charts, bar graphs, histograms
   - Box plots and scatter plots
   - Context and visualization pitfalls
   - Mean, median, percentiles, five-number summary, standard deviation
2. **Producing Data and Sampling**
   - Simple random and stratified random sampling
   - Bias and chance error
   - Observational studies, experiments, confounding, placebo effect
   - Logic of randomized controlled experiments
3. **Probability**
   - Interpretations of probability
   - Complement, equally likely outcomes, addition and multiplication rules
   - “At least one,” total enumeration, Bayes' rule and Bayesian analysis
   - Randomized response
4. **Normal Approximation and Binomial Distribution**
   - Normal curve, empirical rule, standardization
   - Normal approximation and percentiles
   - Binomial setting, coefficient, and formula
   - Random variables, probability histograms
   - Normal approximation to binomial; sampling without replacement
5. **Sampling Distributions and the Central Limit Theorem**
   - Parameter and statistic
   - Expected value and standard error
   - Sums, percentages, simulation, square-root law
   - Sampling distributions and three kinds of histograms
   - Law of Large Numbers and Central Limit Theorem; applicability
6. **Regression**
   - Prediction and correlation
   - Linear association, least-squares line
   - Regression to the mean and regression fallacy
   - Predicting y from x versus x from y
   - Conditional normal approximation
   - Residuals, heteroscedasticity, transformations, outliers, influence
7. **Confidence Intervals**
   - Interpretation of confidence intervals
   - CLT-based intervals
   - Bootstrap estimates of standard error
   - Coverage, confidence level, margin of error
8. **Tests of Significance**
   - Hypotheses, test statistics, p-values
   - Taste-test/binomial testing logic
   - t-test; significance versus importance
   - Two-sample z-test and matched pairs
9. **Resampling**
   - Simulation instead of analytic calculation
   - Monte Carlo and Law of Large Numbers
   - Plug-in principle
   - Parametric bootstrap, bootstrap confidence intervals, regression bootstrap
10. **Analysis of Categorical Data**
    - Relationships between categorical variables
    - Chi-square goodness of fit
    - Chi-square homogeneity and independence
11. **One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)**
    - Comparing several means
    - Within- and between-group variation
    - F statistic and F distribution
12. **Multiple Comparisons**
    - Data snooping and multiple-testing fallacy
    - Reproducibility and replicability
    - Bonferroni correction, false discovery rate, data splitting
